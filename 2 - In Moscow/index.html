Best to see and Do in Moscow

Kremlin

Everyone's heard of the Kremlin but it isn't just the working place of the President. There's actually lots of different parts, including an armoury, a theatre, monastries and so on. The Kremlin Museum's Official Website has a lot more information on what you can find there and about available tickets



Red Square and St Basil’s Cathedral

This needs no explanation, and frankly it's a hard attraction to avoid. Moscow is a city that slowly grew outwards over the course of history and this square finds itself right in the centre as one of the original additions. Extremely beautiful and never fails to take your breath away. Check out St Basil’s Cathedral's Official Website



Ice Skating at VDNH

This is seriously so fun and much cheaper than in the UK so definitely worth it!! Ice skating is such a popular Russian activity and the ice rinks are extremely well looked after. However, be warned – VDNH is the biggest rink in Europe and that is very obvious if you lose track of where your friends skate off to! You can find more information on VDNH's Official Website



Vorobyovy Gory

It’s very serene down by the river and you can also enjoy the cable cars for views across Moscow. If you’re scared of heights there is also a very impressive observation deck from which you can see all of Moscow!



GUM

This is the iconic Russian department store, right on Red Square. It has loads of designer shops but that’s not the main attraction. The ceiling is extremely impressive and the store is very well decorated, especially around Christmas time!! 100% worth visiting. Here is the link to GUM's Official Websitein case you are feeling curious!



Arbat

All though this has turned very touristy, it is still an important and iconic part of Moscow. You can find all your souvenirs here, as well as the Pushkin Apartment Museum. It’s extremely near Red Square/Okhotny Ryad so worth a visit
